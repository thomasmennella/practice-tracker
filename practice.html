Right Effort (Sammā-vāyāma)
Definition & Guidance ▾

Sammā-vāyāma — the four great efforts (cattāro sammappadhānā): preventing unarisen unwholesome states from arising; abandoning arisen unwholesome states; cultivating unarisen wholesome states; maintaining arisen wholesome states.

The operative word in all four is cetanā — intentional mental engagement with the quality of what is arising. Not willpower applied to behavior but attention applied to the mind's own movements.

Right Intention (Sammā-saṅkappa)
Definition & Guidance ▾

Sammā-saṅkappa — three qualities of intention: nekkhamma (renunciation — toward release rather than acquisition); abyāpāda (non-ill-will — goodwill rather than aversion); avihiṃsā (harmlessness — compassion rather than cruelty).

These operate beneath action and speech as motivational ground. Wrong intention can accompany technically correct behavior. The question is not what you did but what was driving it.

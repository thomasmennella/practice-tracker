The Four Primary Nikāyas
DNDīgha Nikāya — the long discourses (34 suttas) MNMajjhima Nikāya — the middle-length discourses (152) SNSaṃyutta Nikāya — connected discourses, grouped by theme (2,889) ANAṅguttara Nikāya — numbered discourses, grouped by count (2,344)
Khuddaka Nikāya — the "Minor Collection" (early strata)
DhpDhammapada — 423 verses on the path; the most beloved UdUdāna — 80 "inspired utterances" of the Buddha ItiItivuttaka — 112 short "thus it was said" teachings SnpSutta Nipāta — among the oldest texts; includes the Aṭṭhaka & Pārāyana ThagTheragāthā — verses of the elder monks ThigTherīgāthā — verses of the elder nuns; earliest women's spiritual poetry KpKhuddakapāṭha — short passages for recitation
Khuddaka Nikāya — narrative & later material
JaJātaka — past-life stories of the Buddha VvVimānavatthu — stories of heavenly rebirths PvPetavatthu — stories of ghost (peta) rebirths PaṭisPaṭisambhidāmagga — analytical treatise NettNettippakaraṇa — guide to interpretation
For a practitioner, the four Nikāyas together with the Sutta Nipāta, Udāna, Itivuttaka, and Dhammapada sit closest to the Buddha's direct teaching. The narrative collections (Jātaka, Vimāna, Peta) are later and more devotional in character.
